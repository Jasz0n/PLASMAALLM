Notice something important:

The AI isn't replacing developers or researchers.

Instead, it becomes the system that remembers everything, connects ideas, finds contradictions, and suggests what should be tested next.

The humans still do what humans are best at:

building prototypes,
running experiments,
making observations,
discovering new ideas.

The AI does what it's best at:

organizing millions of pieces of information,
detecting patterns,
tracking evidence,
identifying gaps in knowledge.

Together, they continuously improve the shared knowledge base.

One idea I think fits your vision very well

Instead of treating every contribution as "a post," treat it as an Evidence Package.

An evidence package might contain:

The claim being investigated.
The design files or code.
Photos or videos of the experiment.
Measurements and raw data.
Hardware or software versions.
Environmental conditions.
Reproduction steps.
Whether the experiment succeeded, failed, or was inconclusive.

The Knowledge Graph would then link every concept to the evidence packages that support or challenge it.

For example:

Concept:
High-Efficiency Energy Converter

Evidence
├── Paper A
├── Workshop Transcript
├── Prototype #1
├── Prototype #2
├── Independent Replication #14
├── Failed Replication #6
└── Debate #42

Nothing is hidden. Future researchers can inspect why the confidence is high or low.

I would make one principle central to the whole ecosystem


Knowledge is earned through transparent evidence, not authority. Confidence increases through reproducible results, not popularity. Every conclusion remains traceable to the observations that support it.

That principle keeps the system open to new ideas without becoming gullible, and grounded in evidence without becoming dogmatic.
